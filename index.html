Research Overview

My research interests lie in the intersection of the pure mathematics and real world system modeling, with applications in social networks, and biology. The goal of my research is to provide a thorough and deep understanding of fundamental limits of (possibly networked) dynamic systems, and develop simple, flexible, efficient, and graceful algorithms that can achieve provably good performance. Until now, my PhD research activities can be organized as follows.

Source node detection in a network

Scaling law analysis in large scale wireless ad hoc network

During my bachelor study, I work on the scaling laws of capacity and energy efficiency in wireless networks.

1) In contrast to previous works, we consider a heterogeneous distribution function to model the location of nodes in the ad hoc network.

2) We not only analyze the scaling law of capacity, but also obtain the scaling results on energy efficiency by studying the average hop counts of a single multicast flow.
